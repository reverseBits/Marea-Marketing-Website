Iron deficiency is the most common nutritional deficiency worldwide, and menstruation is the leading cause in people who menstruate. Despite this, it is routinely under-investigated. The symptoms are pervasive enough to be attributed to stress, poor sleep, or "just being a bit run-down", and a standard blood test often misses it.

### Why periods cause iron deficiency

Every cycle, menstrual blood loss removes iron from the body. A typical period involves losing 30–40ml of blood. Heavy periods, defined clinically as more than 80ml per cycle, or flooding through a pad within an hour, can remove two to three times that amount.

The body can partially compensate through dietary iron absorption, but this only works if dietary iron is adequate and absorption is efficient. If losses consistently exceed replacement, iron stores deplete over time, often over months or years, silently.

### The ferritin-haemoglobin gap

This is the piece most doctors and patients miss. A standard blood test for anaemia checks haemoglobin levels. But haemoglobin only falls when iron deficiency is severe, it is a late marker. Iron stores, measured by ferritin (a storage protein), deplete long before haemoglobin drops.

Someone can have ferritin levels so low that their cells are iron-starved, yet their haemoglobin is technically within the normal range. They will test negative for anaemia but experience every symptom of iron deficiency. This gap between "has anaemia" and "has iron deficiency" is where many people go years without a diagnosis.

### Symptoms that are easy to attribute to other things

Iron deficiency causes fatigue, but not just physical tiredness. It impairs mitochondrial function, which affects energy production at the cellular level. The fatigue of iron deficiency has a specific quality: a heaviness and reduced motivation that does not fully resolve with sleep.

Cognitive fog, difficulty concentrating, and slowed processing speed are well-documented in iron deficiency. So is intolerance to cold, iron is required for thyroid hormone function, and low ferritin affects temperature regulation. Hair shedding, particularly the diffuse hair loss that appears a few months after a stressful period or heavy cycles, is often iron-related.

The overlap with burnout, anxiety, and depression is significant enough that iron deficiency should be checked before any of those diagnoses are made.

### What to eat and how to absorb it better

Haem iron, found in red meat and fish, is absorbed at a rate of approximately 20–30%. Non-haem iron, found in lentils, beans, tofu, dark leafy greens, and fortified foods, is absorbed at 2–10%, considerably less, but improvable.

The single most effective strategy for improving non-haem iron absorption is pairing it with vitamin C at the same meal. Vitamin C converts iron into the more absorbable ferrous form. Adding lemon juice to lentil dal, eating tomatoes with spinach, or having orange juice with a fortified cereal provides meaningful uplift in absorption.

Tannins in tea and coffee and calcium in dairy inhibit iron absorption if consumed with iron-rich meals. Spacing these by an hour makes a measurable difference.

<div className="label">Research finding</div>

A study of adolescent girls in India found iron deficiency in 56% of participants. Globally, approximately 30–40% of adolescent girls are estimated to have iron deficiency or iron deficiency anaemia. A systematic review found that ferritin thresholds used in standard clinical practice frequently miss iron deficiency that is symptomatic and functionally significant, and that symptoms improved with iron supplementation even in people who technically tested within the normal haemoglobin range.

<div className="takeaway-box">
<div className="label">The test to ask for</div>

Ask your doctor to check your **ferritin** level, not just haemoglobin. Specify ferritin. A ferritin below 30 ng/mL is associated with symptoms even without anaemia; below 15 ng/mL is frank deficiency. If your periods are heavy and you have any of the symptoms above, this is the single most useful test to request.
